In power analysis, it is not possible to know the exact values of means before the experiment is observed. Scale factors allow you to consider alternative values for the means by scaling the values entered on the previous screen.
For example, entering the scale factors 0.5, 1, and 2 would compute power for the mean values divided by 2, the mean values as entered, and the mean values multiplied by 2.
